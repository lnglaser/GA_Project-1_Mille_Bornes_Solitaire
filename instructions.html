Mille Bornes Solitaire

Welcome to Mille Bornes Solitaire! This is a single-player version of a game published in France in the 1960s, and brought to America by Parker Brothers.

The object of the game is to complete a trip of exactly 1000 miles. You do this by playing miles to your score, and by dealing with various hazards and obstacles one would encounter on the road.

Game Components

  • Mileage cards - 200 (x4), 100 (x12), 75 (x10), 50 (x10), 25 (x10)
  • Hazard cards - Red light (x5), Out of gas (x3), Flat tire (x3), Accident (x3)
  • Remedy cards - Green light (x14), Gas (x6), Spare tire (x6), Repairs (x6)

How to Play

  • The game cards are dealt into two decks - the player receives a deck containing all the mileage and remedy cards, while the computer receives a deck containing all the hazard cards. The player is dealt six cards from their deck, and then one more card is drawn.
  • The player has a battle pile and a score counter. Remedies and hazards are played to the battle pile, and miles to the score. Only the top card on the battle pile is ever in effect.
  • The battle pile will be started with a Green light card. Miles can only be played while the Green light card is on top of the battle pile.
  • On your turn, after drawing a card from the draw pile you may:
    • Play mileage cards to your score (if your battle pile shows a Green light)
    • Discard a card
    • Play a remedy card over a hazard card on your battle pile
  • Each type of remedy will only remove one type of hazard. The hazards and associated remedies are:
    • Red light, remedied by Green light
    • Out of gas, remedied by Gas
    • Flat tire, remedied by Spare tire
    • Accident, remedied by Repairs
  • Note that even after playing an appropriate remedy on a hazard, you must then play a Green light card on your next turn before you can play any more mileage cards.
  • That's it! If you get to exactly 1000 miles before you run out of turns, you win! Grab your keys, put on your best driving gloves, and let's motor!
